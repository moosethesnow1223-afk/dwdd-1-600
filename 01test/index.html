Digital Citizenship

What is Digital Citizenship?

Digital citizenship refers to the responsible, ethical, and safe use of technology to engage with society.

What is Digital Law?

Digital law refers to the legal principles, regulations, and ethics that govern the use of technology, the internet, and electronic communications. It defines the electronic responsibility for actions and deeds, outlining what is permitted and prohibited in digital environments.

What is AUP?

AUP most commonly stands for Acceptable Use Policy, a set of rules defining how individuals can use an organization's technology (networks, computers, internet, etc.), outlining permitted and prohibited activities to ensure security, compliance, and responsible usage

What is Plagiarism?

Plagiarism is the act of presenting someone else's work, words, or ideas as your own without providing proper credit.

What is Acedemic Dishonesty?

Academic dishonesty refers to any act of deception that misrepresents a student's performance or provides an unfair advantage in an academic exercise.
