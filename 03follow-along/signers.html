Who were the Signers

A total of 56 delegates signed the Declaration of Independence, representing the 13 American colonies. While some are household names, many others are remembered for specific historical roles or personal trivia.

Famous Signers

John Hancock
The President of the Continental Congress who famously provided the largest and most prominent signature on the document.
Thomas Jefferson
The primary author of the Declaration and the third U.S. President.
Benjamin Franklin
A world-renowned polymath, inventor, and diplomat who was the oldest signer at age 70.
John Adams
A key advocate for independence and the second U.S. President.
Samuel Adams
A leader of the Sons of Liberty and a major political figure in revolutionary Boston.
Edward Rutledge & Thomas Lynch Jr.
The youngest signers of the document, both representing South Carolina at age 26.
Charles Carroll of Carrollton
The last surviving signer, who lived until 1832.
Button Gwinnett
A representative from Georgia whose signature is now among the rarest and most valuable in the world.
John Witherspoon
The only active clergyman and minister to sign the Declaration.

Each of thease individuals played a significant role in the founding of the United States and is rembered for their courage in the signing the Declaration, An act of considered treasonous by the British Crown
